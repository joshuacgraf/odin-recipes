Mexican Rice

Description

Mexican rice is a tasty dish in its own right (although it does benefit from soaking up the juices from other elements on a plate). Also known as arroz a la mexicana, arroz mexicano, or arroze rojo, this side dish is made of white rice, tomato, garlic, and onion with other spices and ingredients. This is my non-authentic (but hopefully tasty) take on this classic dish.

Ingredients

  • two table spoons oil
  • 2 cups rice
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 1 cup corn kernels
  • 1 medium yellow onion
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup red wine
  • Cojita cheese for garnish

For the spice mix

  • pinch of oregano
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 2 tbsp chicken bullion
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tsp chili powder
  • 2 tsp ceyenne pepper powder

Directions

  1. Mix spices into bowl.
  2. Blend onion and garlic cloves in blender.
  3. Heat chicken broth and red wine over medium heat.
  4. Place oil in dutch oven over medium heat. Once warm, add briefly rinced rice and stir evenly until medium browned.
  5. Add tomato sauce and mix with rice.
  6. Add blended onion and garlic cloves and mix.
  7. Add spice mix and stir in. Then cook for 1 minute.
  8. Add broth & wine then mix. Place lid on dutch oven and let simmer for 10 minutes without stiring.
  9. Let rice finish with stove off.
  10. Serve with a sprinkle of cojita cheese.
